Lacrosse: The Oldest North American Sport

Posted on January 1, 2023 by Connor Cunningham
Web Design Freebies

What is Lacrosse and Where did it originate from? Well Lacrosse is a Native American sport and the oldest sport in North America.

The Native American tribes used to play the sport for getting young men ready for war, religious reasons, and recreation.

The word “Lacrosse” came from a French Jesuit Missionary named Jean de Brébeuf and it means game of sticks.

The Difficulties of Lacrosse

Lacrosse also isn't a sport you can just pick up and get good, there is a lot more to it. The ball itself is a rubber ball and shots on net can reach up to 80 mph and more (fastest shot ever recorded was 127.4). But that's not all there is precision and stick handling you have to take into account. You can just pick up a stick for the first time and start ripping shots at 80 mph.

You have to train a lot to get to that point. Even just passing the ball to a teammate can be difficult. Me personally as a goalie I think it's fun to stand in the middle of a net and get pelted by rubber balls. Most people can't even think to be a goalie and most goalies are thought to be crazy or you have to be messed up in the head to be a true goalie. But in my opinion it's what I'm best at and it's my passion

Timeline of Lacrosse

12th century The start of Lacrosse is a mystery but some historians say it satrted around the 12th century
1636 In the 1600s French Jesuit Missionaries came to see the Huron(Native Americans) play lacrosse and thus the name "lacrosse" was introduced.
1856 The first Lacrosse club was formed called in Montreal Canada starting the begining of orginized lacrosse
Early 1900s Lacrosse is starting to gain popularity world wide with Universities and Colleges playing the sport in The USA, Australia, The United Kingdom, and Canada
1904/1908 Lacrosse is played for the first time in the Olympics in St. louis, London
1928/1932/1948 Lacrosse is featured as a demonstration game in the Olympics
1972 The International Federation of Womans Lacrosse Association was formed
1974 The International Lacrosse Federation(men) was born
2008 The ILF and The IFWLA was merged into The Federation of International Lacrosse was formed to promote the sport globally
2017 Women's Lacrosse was played at The World Games
2018 FIL received provisional recognition by the International Olympic Committee(IOC)
2019 FIL rebranded to World Lacrosse
2021 World Lacrosse received full recognition from the IOC
